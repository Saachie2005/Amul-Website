Amul`s journey began in Anand, Gujarat, when farmers, exploited by unfair trade practices, formed a cooperative in 1946 under the guidance of Sardar Patel and leaders like Morarji Desai and Tribhuvandas Patel. This led to the creation of the Kaira District Co-operative Milk Producers Union, later known as Amul Dairy. With Dr. Verghese Kurien`s leadership from 1950, Amul grew rapidly. Recognizing its success, Prime Minister Lal Bahadur Shastri adopted the Amul model for national dairy development, leading to the formation of the National Dairy Development Board in 1965. The three-tier Amul model directly links farmers to consumers by eliminating middlemen.
